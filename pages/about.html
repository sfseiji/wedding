Short Biography

I grew up in a rural area of Kyoto, Japan. Surrounded by mountains, my hometown offered endless outdoor adventures that sparked my curiosity about the world. I was passionate about playing baseball until recurring elbow injuries forced me to quit around the age of 15. I then took up badminton in high shcool and Doutble Dutch in university, eventually winning the world championship of Double Dutch in 2014.

Driven by curiosity about the many unknowns of the universe, I pursued graduate studies in astronomy and earned my PhD from the University of Tokyo in 2019. After that, I worked as a Postdoc researcher in Copenhagen (Denmark) and Austin (USA) before joining the faculty at the University of Toronto in 2025. As an observational extragalactic astronomer, I specialize in multi-wavelength observations using large telescopes in the world, such as ALMA and JWST, to study dust, stellar populations, and gas in distant galaxies.

I enjoy traveling, playing sports, photography, camping, and board games. Ultimately, my greatest pleasure lies in enhancing the joyful moments shared with friends.
